## 🧠 The 30-Second Version

The dashboard is split into two halves: **Income** (top) and **Expenses** (bottom). In plain terms:

- **Income came in $3,000 below budget** — the company earned less than it planned to.
- **Expenses came in $15 over budget** — basically right on target, a tiny overspend.
- Both income and expenses are running at **~84–88% of their full-year budget**, meaning the company is earning *and* spending less than planned overall.

**Bottom line:** Spending is well controlled (almost exactly on budget), but revenue is falling short — the real story here is a **shortfall on the income side**, not overspending.

---

## 🗂️ Basic Vocabulary (in plain English)

| Term | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|
| **Budget** | The target/plan number — what was expected to happen |
| **Actual** | What really happened |
| **Difference** | Budget minus Actual (or vice versa) — the gap between plan and reality |
| **% of Budget** | How close actual results are to 100% of the plan. 100% = exactly on target. Below 100% for income = underperforming; below 100% for expenses = spending less than allowed (good) |
| **Current Month / Previous Month** | This month's number vs last month's, so you can see if things are improving or worsening |

## 🚦 How Anyone Should Read This Dashboard (Step-by-Step)

1. **Look at the two headline numbers first.** Income is down $3,000 vs budget (bad); Expenses are up just $15 (basically neutral) — so the real issue is on the income side.
2. **Check the % of Budget bars.** Income is at 88.45% of target; Expenses are at 83.85% of allowance. Both are under 100%, but for Income that's a shortfall, while for Expenses that's actually favorable (spent less than planned).
3. **Scan the monthly charts.** For Income, look for months where the bar falls below the budget line — those are the underperforming months (June, August, October). For Expenses, July is the one big red flag — a huge one-month spike above budget.
4. **Check the category tables.** For Income, every single category is under target (red) — this is a broad-based shortfall, not one bad product. For Expenses, every category is under budget (green) — strong spending discipline everywhere.
5. **Conclusion:** the business is controlling costs well, but needs to investigate **why income is consistently missing target across every category**, and what caused the **July expense spike**.
